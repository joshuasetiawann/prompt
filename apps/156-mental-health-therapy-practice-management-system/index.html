How it works

From intake to invoice in four steps

MindLedger mirrors the real rhythm of a behavioral-health practice — each screen hands off cleanly to the next.

1

Onboard the client

Capture consent, demographics, and an income band, then run the PHQ-9 and GAD-7 baseline in the intake form.

↳ Intake & assessment forms
2

Schedule the work

Set a recurring session on the calendar and let MindLedger resolve the sliding-scale fee for every appointment.

↳ Scheduling calendar
3

Document the session

Open the client chart, write the structured note beside the session timeline, then sign and lock it.

↳ Client chart & note editor
4

Bill & review

Each kept session creates an invoice; record payment and the dashboard flags any overdue plan reviews.

↳ Billing & dashboard

Privacy by design

Sensitive records deserve a careful system

Mental-health data is some of the most sensitive there is. MindLedger scopes every query to the signed-in user and validates every change on the server.

Role-based access controlTherapists, admins, and clients only reach the pages and records their role allows.
Server-side validationEvery mutation is checked on the server, so no malformed clinical record slips through.
Audit & amendment trailSigned notes lock; later edits are recorded as dated amendments, never silent overwrites.
