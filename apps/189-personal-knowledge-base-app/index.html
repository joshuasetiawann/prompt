Everything a second brain needs

Notes that think in connections

Nine building blocks work together so capture stays effortless and nothing you write ever gets lost or stranded.

🔗

Bidirectional wiki-links

Write [[like this]] and MindVault parses the link, builds the connection, and surfaces it in a live backlinks panel on the target note.

📅

Automatic daily notes

Open today and your daily note is already there. Browse the past on a date calendar and scroll a chronological timeline of everything you captured.

🏷️

Nested tag browser

Organize with hierarchical tags like #learning/memory. Filter by a tag and it pulls in every descendant beneath it.

🔍

Full-text search

Search across every note title and body in an instant — always scoped to the notebooks you actually have access to.

🕸️

Interactive graph view

See your knowledge as a graph of notes connected by their links. Pan, zoom, and follow a thread of ideas you forgot you had.

📚

Shareable notebooks

Group related notes into notebooks — a daily journal, a research log, a project — and share any one of them without exposing the rest.

👥

Invite as viewer or editor

Bring collaborators into a notebook by invite with a clear role: viewers read, editors write. Every note access is checked against that role.

⚡

Link autocomplete

Type [[ and pick from suggestions — or create the note on the spot. Linked-but-empty notes are made automatically, so a thought is never a dead end.

🕰️

Per-note revision history

Every save records a revision. Look back at how a note evolved and restore an earlier version whenever you need it.

Who it's for

For anyone building a second brain

MindVault is made for people who capture ideas all day and need them to stay connected — solo, or with a small circle of collaborators.

🧠

Knowledge workers

Turn scattered meeting notes, decisions, and references into a connected vault you can actually search and revisit instead of losing in a dozen docs.

🎓

Students

Link lectures to readings, build study systems with active recall, and let daily notes track what you learned each day across a whole semester.

🔬

Researchers

Keep literature, ideas, and open questions in one graph — then invite a collaborator into a shared notebook to read along or write with you.

Share a notebook, not your whole vault

Invite collaborators into a single notebook with a role that's enforced on every note inside it.

Owner
Creates notes and notebooks, owns the vault, and invites collaborators as editors or viewers. Full control over what gets shared.
Editor
Can read and edit notes inside notebooks shared with them — write, link, and tag freely, without touching the rest of the owner's vault.
Viewer
Gets read-only access to a shared notebook. Perfect for sharing a research log or journal where edits aren't wanted.
